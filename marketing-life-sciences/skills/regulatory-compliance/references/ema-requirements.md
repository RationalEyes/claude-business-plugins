# EMA Advertising and Promotional Requirements

<!-- Life Sciences Addition -->

Reference guide for European Medicines Agency and EU member state regulations governing pharmaceutical promotional materials.

## Governing Framework

### EU-Level Legislation

- **Directive 2001/83/EC** (Title VIII, Articles 86-100) — the primary EU directive governing advertising of medicinal products for human use
- **Directive 2001/83/EC, Article 86(1)** — defines advertising as any form of door-to-door information, canvassing activity, or inducement designed to promote prescribing, supply, sale, or consumption
- **Regulation (EC) No 726/2004** — establishes the centralized procedure and EMA oversight

### Key Principles Under EU Law

- Advertising must comply with the particulars listed in the Summary of Product Characteristics (SmPC)
- Advertising must encourage rational use of the product by presenting it objectively and without exaggerating its properties
- Advertising must not be misleading
- Advertising of prescription-only medicines to the general public is prohibited throughout the EU

## Advertising to Healthcare Professionals

### Required Content (Article 91)

All advertising to HCPs must include:
- Essential information compatible with the SmPC
- The supply classification (prescription-only, pharmacy-only, etc.)
- The date on which the advertisement was drawn up or last revised

### Content Standards

- Every statement must be accurate, up to date, verifiable, and sufficiently complete to enable the HCP to form an opinion of the therapeutic value of the product
- Quotations, tables, and illustrations from medical journals or scientific works must be faithfully reproduced with precise source references
- No misleading omission of material information

### Prohibited Practices (Article 94)

- No gifts, pecuniary advantages, or benefits in kind to HCPs, except when inexpensive and relevant to medical practice
- Hospitality at promotional events must be reasonable in level and subordinate to the scientific purpose
- Hospitality must not extend to persons other than HCPs
- Free samples are limited (Article 96): only during the first year(s) of marketing; limited quantities; marked "free medical sample — not for sale"; compliant with the SmPC

## Advertising to the General Public

### Prohibition for Prescription Medicines

- **Article 88** — advertising to the general public is prohibited for prescription-only medicines and medicines containing psychotropic or narcotic substances
- Disease awareness campaigns are permitted provided they do not make direct or indirect reference to a specific medicinal product

### Permitted Public Advertising (OTC Only)

Non-prescription (over-the-counter) medicines may be advertised to the public if the advertisement:
- Is clearly identified as an advertisement
- Identifies the product clearly as a medicinal product
- Contains essential information for correct use
- Contains a clear, legible invitation to read the package leaflet
- Does not contain material that suggests that medical consultation or surgery is unnecessary
- Does not suggest that effects of the medicine are guaranteed or without side effects
- Is not directed mainly at children

## Member State Variations

EU directives set minimum standards. Individual member states often impose additional requirements:

### Germany

- **Heilmittelwerbegesetz (HWG)** — German Medicinal Advertising Act implements Directive 2001/83/EC with additional restrictions
- Advertising must include the text: "Zu Risiken und Nebenwirkungen lesen Sie die Packungsbeilage und fragen Sie Ihren Arzt oder Apotheker" (for public advertising)
- The FSA (Freiwillige Selbstkontrolle fur die Arzneimittelindustrie) provides self-regulatory oversight

### France

- **ANSM (Agence nationale de securite du medicament)** pre-approves advertising materials before dissemination (visa system)
- Advertising must be submitted to ANSM for prior authorization (visa PM for promotional materials)
- The Bertrand Law (Loi Bertrand) / Transparence Sante requires public disclosure of all advantages provided to HCPs

### United Kingdom (Post-Brexit)

- **MHRA** regulates advertising under the Human Medicines Regulations 2012 (SI 2012/1916)
- **PMCPA (Prescription Medicines Code of Practice Authority)** administers the ABPI Code of Practice — the primary self-regulatory body
- The ABPI Code requires pre-approval of promotional materials by a certified signatory
- Digital and social media advertising falls under the same code requirements

### Italy

- **AIFA (Agenzia Italiana del Farmaco)** oversees advertising compliance
- Prior authorization required for advertising materials directed to HCPs
- The Farmindustria Code provides self-regulatory standards

### Spain

- Advertising governed by Royal Decree 1416/1994 and enforced by AEMPS
- The Farmaindustria Code of Practice provides self-regulation
- Prior notification (not approval) required for certain promotional activities

## SmPC Compliance Requirements

All promotional claims must be consistent with the approved Summary of Product Characteristics:

| SmPC Section | Promotional Relevance |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Section 4.1 (Therapeutic indications) | All promotional claims must be within approved indications |
| Section 4.2 (Posology and method of administration) | Dosing claims must match approved posology |
| Section 4.3 (Contraindications) | Must be communicated in promotional materials |
| Section 4.4 (Special warnings and precautions) | Fair balance requires inclusion of material warnings |
| Section 4.8 (Undesirable effects) | Adverse event profile must be presented fairly |
| Section 5.1 (Pharmacodynamic properties) | Efficacy data in promotions must match SmPC data |

## EFPIA Code of Practice

The European Federation of Pharmaceutical Industries and Associations (EFPIA) Code provides industry-wide self-regulation:

### Key EFPIA Requirements

- All promotional materials must be accurate, balanced, fair, objective, and sufficiently complete
- Promotional claims must be based on an up-to-date evaluation of all relevant evidence
- Promotional materials must not be misleading by distortion, exaggeration, undue emphasis, omission, or other means
- The word "safe" must not be used without qualification
- The word "new" must not be used to describe any product or presentation that has been generally available for more than 12 months

### Transparency and Disclosure

- EFPIA Disclosure Code requires companies to publicly disclose transfers of value to HCPs and healthcare organizations
- Disclosure covers: fees for services, sponsorship, donations, grants, and contributions to costs of events
- Disclosures published annually on company websites or central platforms

## Digital Advertising in the EU

### General Principles

- The same rules that apply to traditional media apply to digital and social media
- Promotional websites must be clearly identified as pharmaceutical company websites
- Access to promotional content for HCPs should be gated (accessible only to verified HCPs)
- Patient-facing digital content must comply with the prohibition on prescription drug advertising to the public

### Platform-Specific Considerations

- Social media posts making product claims must include risk information or a clear link to it
- Paid digital advertising must comply with the same standards as print advertising
- Cookie consent and data privacy requirements (GDPR) apply to all digital promotional activities
- Email marketing to HCPs requires opt-in consent under GDPR
