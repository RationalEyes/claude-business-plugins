Detailed timeline, checklist, and best practices for planning life sciences marketing and medical affairs activities at medical congresses.

## Planning Timeline

### 12 Months Before Congress

- [ ] Confirm congress participation (go/no-go decision)
- [ ] Reserve exhibition space (booth allocation)
- [ ] Identify data readouts and publications aligned with congress timing
- [ ] Begin budget planning (booth, symposia, travel, materials, entertainment)
- [ ] Identify abstract submission deadlines

### 9 Months Before Congress

- [ ] Submit abstracts for poster and oral presentations
- [ ] Begin satellite symposium planning (if applicable)
- [ ] Identify KOLs for engagement at congress
- [ ] Reserve hotel blocks for company attendees
- [ ] Begin creative brief for booth design

### 6 Months Before Congress

- [ ] Confirm abstract acceptances and presentation slots
- [ ] Finalize satellite symposium program, speakers, and topics
- [ ] Begin development of congress materials (booth panels, handouts, digital assets)
- [ ] Submit congress materials for MLR review
- [ ] Plan KOL meeting schedule (1:1 appointments)
- [ ] Develop social media plan for congress coverage

### 3 Months Before Congress

- [ ] Finalize booth design and construction timeline
- [ ] Complete MLR review of all congress materials
- [ ] Finalize speaker training for satellite symposia
- [ ] Confirm KOL meeting schedule
- [ ] Brief field force on congress data and key messages
- [ ] Plan competitive intelligence gathering strategy
- [ ] Prepare media briefing materials (if data presentation warrants press coverage)
- [ ] Coordinate with medical affairs on MSL congress activities

### 1 Month Before Congress

- [ ] Ship booth materials and equipment
- [ ] Distribute final congress guide to all attendees (schedule, contacts, key sessions)
- [ ] Confirm all logistics (travel, hotels, meeting rooms, dinners)
- [ ] Pre-schedule social media content
- [ ] Prepare competitive intelligence templates and assignments
- [ ] Brief all booth staff on key messages, compliance rules, and escalation procedures

### During Congress

- [ ] Set up booth and verify all materials
- [ ] Execute KOL meeting schedule
- [ ] Attend and document key competitor presentations
- [ ] Conduct satellite symposium
- [ ] Post real-time social media updates (compliant, pre-approved content)
- [ ] Capture booth traffic and lead data
- [ ] Hold daily team briefings to share insights and adjust strategy
- [ ] Document competitive intelligence from competitor sessions and booths

### Post-Congress (Within 2 Weeks)

- [ ] Compile congress report: key data, competitive insights, KOL feedback
- [ ] Distribute congress highlights to broader organization
- [ ] Follow up on KOL meetings (MSL team)
- [ ] Submit congress materials for post-event archiving and compliance documentation
- [ ] Develop content repurposing plan (infographics, webinars, email summaries)
- [ ] Analyze engagement metrics (booth traffic, symposium attendance, social media)
- [ ] Update competitive intelligence files
- [ ] Begin planning for next year's congress

## Booth Strategy

### Booth Staffing Plan

| Role | Responsibility | Number |
|------|---------------|--------|
| Booth lead | Overall coordination, escalation point | 1 |
| Medical affairs / MSLs | Scientific questions, KOL engagement | 2-4 |
| Commercial team | Product information, lead capture | 2-4 |
| Compliance | On-site compliance oversight | 1 (or on-call) |
| Technical support | Device demonstrations, digital tools | As needed |

### Booth Content Rules

- All booth materials must be MLR-approved before the congress
- No unapproved materials may be displayed or distributed
- Staff must be trained on approved messages and compliant response to off-label questions
- Lead capture forms must comply with data privacy regulations (GDPR for EU congresses)
- Competitor product materials should not be visible at your booth

### Booth Traffic Optimization

- Schedule KOL visits during lower-traffic periods for quality engagement
- Use digital tools (touchscreens, iPads) for interactive data exploration
- Offer practical takeaways: dosing cards, treatment algorithms, clinical resources
- Position high-interest content (new data presentations) prominently
- Staff the booth during peak hours (morning breaks, lunch, poster sessions)

## Satellite Symposium Planning

### Program Design

- Duration: typically 60-90 minutes (lunch or evening slot)
- Format: 2-3 presentations with moderated discussion
- Speakers: mix of Tier 1 KOLs and emerging opinion leaders
- Content: must be fair-balanced and scientifically rigorous even when commercially sponsored

### Speaker Management

- Select speakers based on scientific expertise, not prescribing volume
- Provide approved slide decks; speakers may customize within approved parameters
- Conduct speaker training call 2-4 weeks before the event
- Speakers must disclose their relationship with the sponsoring company
- Compensation at fair market value, documented and reported

### Compliance Requirements

- Clearly label the symposium as industry-sponsored
- Separate from official congress scientific sessions in the program
- No product promotion during CME-accredited sessions
- Meals and hospitality subordinate to the scientific purpose
- Record attendance for transparency reporting

## Competitive Intelligence at Congresses

### Intelligence Gathering Framework

Assign team members to cover competitor sessions and capture:
- New clinical data and trial results
- Label-expansion signals from new indication data
- Pipeline updates and new mechanism presentations
- Messaging and positioning changes in competitor booths
- KOL relationships and endorsements observed

### Post-Congress Competitive Debrief

Hold a structured debrief within one week of the congress:
1. Review all competitive intelligence gathered
2. Assess impact on your product positioning
3. Identify messaging or strategy adjustments needed
4. Update competitive battlecards and briefing materials
5. Communicate key insights to leadership and field force
