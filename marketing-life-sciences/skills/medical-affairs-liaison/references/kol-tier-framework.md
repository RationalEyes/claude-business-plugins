# KOL Tier Framework

<!-- Life Sciences Addition -->

Detailed scoring methodology for identifying, evaluating, and tiering Key Opinion Leaders in a therapeutic area.

## Scoring Dimensions

Rate each KOL candidate on the following dimensions using a 1-5 scale. Weight each dimension based on your strategic priorities.

### 1. Publication Impact (Weight: 20-25%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | First/last author on 10+ publications in top-tier journals in the last 5 years; H-index in top decile for the specialty |
| 4 | First/last author on 5-9 publications in high-impact journals; strong citation record |
| 3 | Regular author (3-5 publications in 5 years); mix of high- and mid-tier journals |
| 2 | Occasional author (1-2 publications in 5 years); mid-tier journals |
| 1 | Minimal publication activity in the therapeutic area |

### 2. Clinical Trial Leadership (Weight: 15-20%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | PI or steering committee member on multiple Phase 3 or pivotal trials in the therapeutic area |
| 4 | PI on Phase 2-3 trials; active clinical research program |
| 3 | Sub-investigator on Phase 2-3 trials; involved in investigator-initiated trials |
| 2 | Clinical trial site involvement without leadership role |
| 1 | No significant clinical trial involvement |

### 3. Congress Presence (Weight: 15-20%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Invited plenary or keynote speaker at major international congresses (ASCO, ESMO, AHA, etc.) |
| 4 | Regular abstract presenter and session chair at major congresses |
| 3 | Poster presenter at major congresses; oral presenter at regional meetings |
| 2 | Occasional congress participation; poster presentations |
| 1 | Minimal congress activity |

### 4. Guideline and Society Involvement (Weight: 15-20%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Chair or lead author of major treatment guidelines (NCCN, ACC/AHA, ESMO, etc.) |
| 4 | Member of guideline writing committees; society board member |
| 3 | Reviewer or contributor to guidelines; active society member |
| 2 | Society member with committee involvement |
| 1 | Basic society membership only |

### 5. Institutional Influence (Weight: 10-15%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Department chair or division chief at a top academic medical center; training program director |
| 4 | Section head or associate professor at an academic center; influences institutional protocols |
| 3 | Attending physician at an academic center; recognized expertise within institution |
| 2 | Community hospital leader or large group practice opinion leader |
| 1 | Individual practitioner without institutional leadership role |

### 6. Digital and Media Influence (Weight: 5-10%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Large social media following (10K+ HCP followers); active medical educator online; media spokesperson |
| 4 | Moderate social media presence (5-10K); regular content creator; occasional media appearances |
| 3 | Active on medical social platforms; participates in online discussions |
| 2 | Limited but present social media or online activity |
| 1 | No meaningful digital presence |

### 7. Engagement Potential (Weight: 10-15%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Known willingness to engage with industry; prior positive advisory board or consulting experience |
| 4 | Open to engagement; has participated in industry activities |
| 3 | Neutral; no strong positive or negative signals |
| 2 | Limited past engagement; some hesitation signals |
| 1 | Known reluctance to engage with industry; anti-industry stance |

## Tier Assignment

### Composite Score Calculation

Composite Score = SUM (Dimension Score x Weight) for all 7 dimensions

### Tier Thresholds

| Tier | Composite Score Range | Approximate % of KOL Pool |
|------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Tier 1 (National/Global) | 4.0 - 5.0 | Top 5-10% |
| Tier 2 (Regional) | 3.0 - 3.9 | Next 15-20% |
| Tier 3 (Local/Community) | 2.0 - 2.9 | Next 25-30% |
| Not tiered | Below 2.0 | Remaining |

### Tier Validation

After scoring, validate tier assignments by asking:
- Does this KOL's tier match the MSL team's field experience?
- Would other KOLs in the field agree with this assessment?
- Are there KOLs who score lower but are emerging stars (rising KOLs)?
- Are there KOLs who score high on metrics but are disengaged or hostile?

Adjust tiers based on qualitative input. The scoring framework provides a starting point, not a final answer.

## KOL Landscape Mapping

### Network Visualization

Map KOL relationships to understand influence networks:
- Co-authorship connections (who publishes together)
- Institutional affiliations (who trains whom)
- Congress co-presentations (who presents together)
- Guideline committee co-membership (who serves together)

### Geographic Distribution

Map KOLs geographically to:
- Align MSL territories with KOL density
- Identify underserved regions where engagement is needed
- Plan regional advisory boards and events
- Support clinical trial site selection

### Emerging KOL Identification

In addition to established KOLs, track emerging opinion leaders:
- Junior faculty with strong publication trajectories
- Early-career researchers winning grants in the therapeutic area
- Physicians active in medical education and social media
- Clinical fellows at top institutions who will become independent investigators
