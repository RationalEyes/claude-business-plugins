# Medical Affairs Liaison Skill

<!-- Life Sciences Addition -->

Frameworks for planning MSL engagement, mapping and tiering KOLs, organizing advisory boards, and developing congress strategies. Medical affairs operates at the intersection of science and commercial strategy — all activities must maintain scientific integrity while supporting business objectives.

## MSL Engagement Planning

Medical Science Liaisons (MSLs) are field-based medical affairs professionals who engage HCPs in non-promotional scientific exchange. MSL activities are distinct from sales activities and must remain non-promotional.

### MSL vs. Sales Representative: Key Distinctions

| Dimension | MSL | Sales Representative |
|-----------|-----|---------------------|
| Primary function | Scientific exchange | Promotional activity |
| Reporting line | Medical affairs | Commercial/Sales |
| Content | Published data, scientific information | Approved promotional materials only |
| Initiation | Can proactively discuss on-label science; reactive to off-label questions | Proactive promotion within approved labeling |
| Compensation | Not tied to prescribing behavior | May include incentives tied to territory performance |
| Regulatory | Can discuss published off-label data reactively | Restricted to approved labeling |

### MSL Territory Planning

Plan MSL territories based on scientific engagement needs, not commercial opportunity:

**Territory sizing factors:**
- Number of Tier 1 and Tier 2 KOLs in the geography
- Academic medical centers and research institutions
- Active clinical trial sites
- Congress and meeting density
- Travel logistics and geography

**Target interaction frequency:**
- Tier 1 KOLs: quarterly interactions (4-6 per year)
- Tier 2 KOLs: semi-annual interactions (2-3 per year)
- Tier 3 KOLs: annual interaction or as needed
- Clinical trial investigators: as needed based on trial milestones

### MSL Interaction Planning

For each planned MSL-KOL interaction, define:
- **Objective** — what scientific exchange is the goal?
- **Key topics** — which data, publications, or scientific questions to discuss
- **Materials** — approved scientific resources, reprints, or data presentations
- **Follow-up** — commitments made, questions to answer, next interaction timing
- **Documentation** — record the interaction in the MSL CRM for compliance

### MSL Activity Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| KOL interactions per quarter | Total scientific exchanges conducted | 40-60 per MSL |
| Tier 1 KOL coverage | % of Tier 1 KOLs contacted per quarter | >90% |
| Interaction quality score | Internal rating of exchange depth and value | Track trend over time |
| Insights captured | Actionable insights documented from interactions | 5-10 per quarter per MSL |
| Medical information requests handled | Unsolicited questions answered | Track volume and response time |
| Congress attendance and coverage | Congresses attended, sessions covered | Per plan |

## KOL Mapping and Tiering

Identify and prioritize Key Opinion Leaders in a therapeutic area. KOL mapping informs MSL deployment, advisory board composition, speaker program selection, and publication strategy.

### KOL Identification Sources

- **Publications** — PubMed, Google Scholar, Scopus. Authors with high publication volume and citation counts in the therapeutic area.
- **Clinical trials** — ClinicalTrials.gov. Principal investigators on relevant trials.
- **Congress presentations** — Abstract authors and invited speakers at major congresses.
- **Guideline committees** — Authors of treatment guidelines (NCCN, ACC/AHA, ADA, ESMO, etc.).
- **Editorial boards** — Members of editorial boards for key journals in the therapy area.
- **Peer nomination** — Ask known KOLs who else they consider influential.
- **Social media and digital influence** — Active commentators on medical Twitter/X, podcast hosts, medical education content creators.

### KOL Tiering Framework

Tier KOLs based on influence, activity, and engagement potential. See `references/kol-tier-framework.md` for the detailed scoring methodology.

**Summary tiers:**

| Tier | Profile | Engagement Level | Typical Activities |
|------|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Tier 1 (National/Global) | Guideline authors, major congress speakers, high-impact publication authors | Highest priority | Advisory boards, speaker programs, publication collaborations, steering committees |
| Tier 2 (Regional) | Regional congress speakers, department heads, active clinical researchers | High priority | Speaker programs, regional advisory boards, clinical trial investigators |
| Tier 3 (Local/Community) | High-prescribing community physicians, local thought leaders | Standard priority | Speaker programs, community education events, practice insights |

### KOL Profiling

For each identified KOL, build a profile that captures:
- **Professional background** — institution, title, specialty, subspecialty
- **Research focus** — active research areas, funded grants, clinical trials
- **Publication record** — key publications, citation metrics, co-author network
- **Congress activity** — presentations, session chairs, poster contributions
- **Guideline involvement** — committee memberships, guideline authorship
- **Industry relationships** — current and past advisory board memberships, consulting, speaker engagements (check Sunshine Act/EFPIA disclosures)
- **Digital presence** — social media activity, podcast participation, online education
- **Sentiment** — attitude toward your company, product, or therapeutic approach
- **Engagement history** — past interactions with your MSL team, advisory boards attended, publications supported

## Advisory Board Planning

Advisory boards bring together KOLs to provide independent expert guidance on scientific, clinical, and strategic questions. They are a core medical affairs activity but carry significant compliance requirements.

### Advisory Board Planning Checklist

1. **Define the objective** — what specific questions do you need expert input on?
2. **Justify the need** — document why an advisory board (vs. other formats) is necessary
3. **Select participants** — choose based on expertise relevant to the objective, not prescribing volume
4. **Set fair market value compensation** — use documented FMV benchmarks for the specialty and time commitment
5. **Prepare the agenda** — structured discussion topics with specific questions for the advisors
6. **Ensure compliance review** — legal and compliance must approve the plan, participant list, and contract terms
7. **Document outcomes** — capture all insights, recommendations, and action items
8. **Demonstrate utilization** — show how the advisory board input influenced decisions (required for compliance)

### Advisory Board Formats

| Format | Size | Duration | Best For |
|--------|------|----------|----------|
| In-person advisory board | 8-15 advisors | Half-day to full day | Deep strategic discussions, complex topics |
| Virtual advisory board | 6-12 advisors | 2-4 hours | Focused topics, geographically dispersed experts |
| Ad hoc consulting | 1-3 advisors | 1-2 hours | Specific tactical questions, rapid input |
| Standing advisory committee | 8-12 advisors | 2-4 meetings per year | Ongoing strategic guidance, longitudinal input |

### Compliance Requirements for Advisory Boards

- Participants must be selected for their expertise, not their prescribing potential
- Compensation must be at fair market value — documented and defensible
- The number of advisors must be justified by the scope of topics
- The venue must be conducive to the meeting purpose (no resort locations without business justification)
- Meals and hospitality must be modest and incidental to the meeting
- All contracts must be executed before the meeting
- Outcomes must be documented and demonstrably utilized
- Transparency reporting required (Sunshine Act, EFPIA Disclosure Code)

## Congress Strategy

Medical congresses are the primary venue for scientific data dissemination, KOL engagement, and competitive intelligence in life sciences. See `references/congress-planning-guide.md` for the detailed planning timeline and checklist.

### Congress Engagement Model

| Activity | Led By | Objective |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| Scientific presentations (posters, orals) | Medical affairs / R&D | Data dissemination |
| Satellite symposia | Medical affairs + Commercial | Education and awareness |
| Exhibition booth | Commercial + Medical | Product information, HCP engagement |
| KOL 1:1 meetings | MSL team | Scientific exchange, relationship building |
| Competitive intelligence | Medical affairs + Commercial | Assess competitor data and strategy |
| Media engagement | Corporate communications | Press coverage of data presentations |
| Social media coverage | Commercial (compliant) | Amplify data presentations, share congress highlights |

### Congress Prioritization

Prioritize congresses based on:
- Relevance to your therapeutic area and product
- KOL attendance density
- Abstract acceptance and data presentation opportunities
- Competitive intelligence value
- Investment required (booth size, symposia, travel)

### Post-Congress Activities

- Distribute congress highlights and data summaries to the field force (after MLR review)
- Update competitive intelligence files with new data
- Follow up on KOL meetings with MSL team
- Repurpose content: infographics, webinars, blog posts summarizing key takeaways
- Analyze engagement metrics: booth traffic, symposia attendance, social media impressions
