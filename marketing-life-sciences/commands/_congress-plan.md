# Congress Plan

<!-- Life Sciences Addition -->

> If you see unfamiliar placeholders or need to check which tools are connected, see [CONNECTORS.md](../CONNECTORS.md).

Generate a comprehensive congress marketing and medical affairs plan for a medical conference, including booth strategy, KOL engagement, data dissemination, competitive intelligence, and post-congress activities.

## Trigger

User runs `/congress-plan` or asks to plan a medical congress, prepare for a scientific conference, develop a congress strategy, or organize conference marketing activities.

## Inputs

Gather the following from the user. If not provided, ask before proceeding:

1. **Congress name** — the specific congress (e.g., ASCO 2026, ESMO 2026, ADA 2026) or therapeutic area if the congress is not yet selected

2. **Product(s)** — which product(s) will be featured, including:
   - Brand name and INN
   - Approved indication(s)
   - Data to be presented (new data, previously presented, post-hoc analyses)
   - Abstract acceptance status (accepted, submitted, planned)

3. **Objectives** — what the company aims to achieve at this congress:
   - Data dissemination (new pivotal data, supportive data, real-world evidence)
   - KOL engagement (advisory meetings, 1:1 scientific discussions)
   - Competitive intelligence (monitor competitor data and activities)
   - Brand awareness (for launched products)
   - Pre-launch preparation (for pipeline products)

4. **Budget range** (optional) — approximate budget for congress activities

5. **Team size** (optional) — number of attendees from the company (MSLs, commercial, medical, leadership)

6. **Historical context** (optional) — what was done at the previous year's congress, what worked and what did not

## Congress Plan Structure

### 1. Congress Overview
- Congress name, dates, location, expected attendance
- Therapeutic area relevance and why this congress matters for the brand
- Key competitor presence expected

### 2. Data Dissemination Plan
For each data presentation:
- Abstract title and presentation format (poster, oral, late-breaking)
- Session date and time
- Presenting author
- Key data points and messaging implications
- Pre-congress communication plan (embargoed media, KOL alerts)
- Post-presentation content plan (press release, social media, email)

### 3. Exhibition Booth Plan
- Booth size and location recommendation
- Booth design themes and key messages
- Interactive elements (touchscreens, MOA demonstrations, clinical tools)
- Staffing plan by role and shift (MSLs, commercial, medical information)
- Staff training requirements and timeline
- Materials checklist (all requiring MLR approval):
  - Booth panels and signage
  - Leave-behind materials
  - Digital content for interactive displays
  - Product samples (if applicable)

### 4. Satellite Symposium Plan (if applicable)
- Topic and scientific justification
- Proposed speakers (with rationale for selection)
- Program format and duration
- Audience target and expected attendance
- Speaker training timeline
- Compliance requirements and disclosures

### 5. KOL Engagement Plan
- Priority KOLs for 1:1 meetings (Tier 1 and Tier 2)
- Meeting objectives for each KOL
- Scheduling approach and meeting format
- MSL assignments
- Topics for discussion (scientific exchange, advisory input, data review)
- Follow-up plan for post-congress engagement

### 6. Competitive Intelligence Plan
- Key competitor presentations to attend
- Competitor booth reconnaissance assignments
- Intelligence gathering template (what to capture, from whom)
- Real-time communication plan for sharing competitive insights
- Post-congress competitive intelligence report timeline

### 7. Social Media and Communications Plan
- Pre-congress content calendar (countdown posts, session highlights)
- During-congress content plan (live coverage, key takeaway posts)
- Post-congress content plan (recap posts, data summaries)
- Compliance review process for real-time content
- Hashtag strategy and platform priorities
- Media engagement plan (press briefings, interviews)

### 8. Content Repurposing Plan
Post-congress content to develop:
- Congress highlights email to field force and broader organization
- Data summary infographics for HCP distribution
- Webinar series recapping key presentations
- Blog posts or articles summarizing congress takeaways
- Updated competitive intelligence briefings
